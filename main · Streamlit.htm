Parkinson's Disease Symptoms Detailed Description:

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement. Here are some common symptoms and their meanings:

  • Tremor: Tremor is an involuntary shaking movement that usually begins in a limb.
  • Bradykinesia: Bradykinesia refers to slowness of movement and is one of the cardinal symptoms of Parkinson's disease.
  • Rigidity: Rigidity is stiffness or inflexibility of the muscles.
  • Postural instability: Postural instability is difficulty maintaining an upright position and balance.
  • Low Blood Pressure: Low blood pressure, or hypotension, is a drop in blood pressure levels that can cause dizziness and fainting.
  • Impaired balance: Impaired balance is the inability to maintain a steady and stable posture.
  • Micrographia: Micrographia is a characteristic handwriting symptom where the writing becomes progressively smaller and more cramped.
  • Constipation: Constipation is a common gastrointestinal symptom in Parkinson's disease.
  • Dystonia: Dystonia is a movement disorder characterized by involuntary muscle contractions.
  • Drooling: Drooling, or sialorrhea, is excessive saliva production and difficulty swallowing.
  • Speech changes: Speech changes in Parkinson's disease include soft, monotone, and slurred speech.
  • Swallowing difficulties: Swallowing difficulties, or dysphagia, occur when it becomes hard to move food or liquid from the mouth to the stomach.
